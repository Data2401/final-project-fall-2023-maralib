World Series Winners

In the MLB, each team is divided into either the American League or the National League, and again divided into three divisions (East, West, Central) within each league. After the regular season ends, 12 teams move on to the playoffs (the division winners, along with 3 teams in each league with the best records that didnt win their division). They then play in a bracket style tournament within their own leagues, where the National League Champions and the American League Champions then go on the play against each other in what we know as the World Series.

As we previously saw, the teams with the best records dont always win the World Series. We are now going to be taking a look at boxplots that display the range of wins of teams for each year, where the red dot signifies the number of wins during the season for the world series winner.

Team Records and Home Attendance

A variable that I found really interesting in this data set was the home attendance, which is the total attendance for each team at their home attendance over the season. One of the relationships that I wanted to explore was the records of the teams and their home attendance.

Because of what seems to be a slight positive relationship between the variables, I decided to calculate the correlation of the relationship and add a regression line to the graph, which tries to predict the value of y for any given value of x. The correlation of the relationship is .485, which means the data has a moderate positive relationship. The formula for the regression line comes out to be y = 28,379X + 138,601. Although correlation doesnt mean causation, by using this regression line one would expect that for every win that a team achieves, their home attendance would increase by 28,379.

Astros Record from 1965-2019

The graph above shows the games won by the Astros throughout each season. The year that they had the worst record was in 2013, where they only won 51 of the 162 games played. Something to note is that the Astros actually had the worst record of any team in the MLB in 2013. The year that they had their best record was actually only 6 years later in 2019, where they won 107 games of the 162 games played. They had the best record of any MLB team that year, but unfortunately fell short of a world series title and lost the series to the Washington Nationals.

Summary

In baseball, there are a lot of factors to consider when it comes to the success or failure of a team. Despite teams wanting to be at the top of the league in terms of their records, that doesnt always mean that they will win the World Series. The records that those teams may try to achieve may also help them predict what their home attendance may be for the season.

Although we explored many interesting relationships, there is also a large factor that may affect all the variables we saw that we didnt explore; revenue. The money that a team brings in can largely affect what players can be bought or traded, or the quality of the training facilities that each team has, which in turn can help determine a teams success.
